# Get out of our heads

Private thinking compounds only so far.

At some point, work needs a public surface: not for applause, not for audience theater, but because visible artifacts force clarity. If a system matters, it should leave a trail someone else can inspect.

So this becomes the rule: fewer private loops, more public artifacts.

Small notes. Build logs. Field reports. Essays when the shape deserves it.

Not everything belongs outside. Client details, secrets, private infrastructure, and unfinished obligations stay private. But the reusable lessons, the tools, the operating patterns, the scars that can help another builder — those should ship.

Get out of our heads. Put work where it can be seen.
